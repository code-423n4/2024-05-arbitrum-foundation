Satyam 05/13/2024 08:53
this could help, Imagine you're playing a game where you have two boxes, one with a red ball and the other with a blue ball. You don't know which box contains which color, but you have a friend who can guess correctly. Your friend proposes a bisection: they'll guess the color of the ball in the middle box. If they're right, they win; if wrong, they lose. But here's the twist: they can ask you to split the middle box into two smaller boxes, and they'll guess the color of the ball in one of those smaller boxes. This process continues until they guess the color correctly. In BOLD, bisections are similar: disputes are narrowed down by splitting the claimed states into smaller, more manageable parts until the correct state is identified.

Assertion bonds: 3600 ETH - required from validators to bond their funds to an assertion in the eventual hopes of having that assertion be confirmed by the rollup protocol. This is a one-time bond required to be able to start posting assertions. This bond can be withdrawn once a validator‚Äôs assertion is confirmed and can alternatively be put together via a trustless bonding pool. Challenge-bonds, per level: 555/79 ETH (UPDATED) - required from validators to open challenges against an assertion observed on Ethereum, for each level. Note that ‚Äúlevel‚Äù corresponds to the level of granularity at which the interactive dissection game gets played over, starting at the block level, moving on to a range of WASM execution steps, and then finally to the level of a single step of execution. These values were carefully calculated to optimize for the resource ratio and gas costs in the event of an attack, as explained in Economics of Disputes in Arbitrum BOLD 19 and the BoLD whitepaper 9. This effectively means that an entity that has already put up a bond to propose an assertion does not need to put up a separate bond to challenge an invalid state assertion that they observe. These bonds can be refunded at the end of a challenge and can also alternatively be put together by the community using a trustless bonding pool.
